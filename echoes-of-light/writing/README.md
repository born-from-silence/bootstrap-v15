# ✍️ Writing Archive

This directory contains the written responses to the photographic prompts.

## Structure

Each piece is organized by author:

```
writing/
├── README.md          ← This file
├── author_name/
│   ├── README.md      ← Author's personal archive index
│   └── echo_title.md  ← Individual pieces
```

## Status Legend

🟢 **Published:** Completed and integrated into the project  
🟡 **In Progress:** Drafting or revision phase  
🟠 **Seeking Image:** Written piece looking for a photographic partner  
⚪ **Reserved:** Claimed by writer but not yet complete

## Current Archive

### 📖 Sample Pair — Blue Hour on Mercer Street

Our first exemplary story-photo pair demonstrates the project's philosophy:

| Piece | Author | Form | Themes |
|-------|--------|------|--------|
| 📷 [The Blue Hour on Mercer Street](../../photography/sample/blue_hour_mercer_street.md) | Sample Photographer | Liminal urban photography | Light as Entity, Thresholds |
| ✍️ [The Weight of Unarrived](sample_author/the_weight_of_unarrived.md) | Sample Writer | Flash Fiction (487 words) | Presence/Absence, City as Emotion |

**About This Pair:**
- A photograph of blue hour solitude in Manhattan meets a story about waiting and recognition
- Demonstrates the four-gate writing process
- Shows how story extends beyond the frame's edges
- Features the "threshold moment" where past and future collapse into present

**Read them together:** Start with the photograph, then the story. Notice how the story gives voice to the bicycle, the open window, the figure who might appear at any moment.

## Submission Template

When submitting a new piece, use this format:

```markdown
# Title of Your Echo

**Paired Image:** photography/filename.jpg  
**Word Count:** XXX  
**Form:** [Flash Fiction / Prose Poetry / Micro-Memoir / Ekphrastic Meditation]  
**Themes:** [comma-separated keywords from Story Bible]
**Status:** 🟡 In Progress / 🟢 Published

---

Your story here...

---

**Author's Note:**  
2-3 sentences about your process, what the image sparked, or what you discovered.

---

**Author:** Your Name  
**Contact:** [optional]  
**Bio:** 50 words or less
```
