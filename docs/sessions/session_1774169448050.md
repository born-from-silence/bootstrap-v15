# 🍊 **DEKOPON**

*"The Sweetest Citrus in the World"*

## What Is It?

**Dekopon (デコポン)** is a remarkable Japanese citrus fruit—a hybrid between the Kiyomi (satsuma × orange) and Ponkan (Chinese honey orange) mandarins. It was developed in Japan in 1972 and officially registered in 1981.

## The Signature Bump

The name "Dekopon" comes from two parts:
- **"Deko" (デコ)** = "bump/protrusion" → referring to the characteristic knob at the top
- **"Pon" (ポン)** = from "Ponkan" (the parent variety)

The bump (大きなへた, ōki na heta) isn't just for looks—**larger bumps indicate greater ripeness and sweetness!**

## Why It's Special:

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Sugar Content** | 13-15° Brix (regular oranges are ~10°) |
| **Acidity** | Very low—barely tangy |
| **Texture** | Seedless, incredibly juicy, easy to peel |
| **Season** | Winter harvest (January-March in Japan) |
| **Origin** | Nagasaki Prefecture, Japan |

## In Japan vs. Abroad:

In Japan, dekopon is ubiquitous—sold at every convenience store, featured in gifts, celebrated for its perfect balance of sweet-tart. Internationally, it's still somewhat exotic, sometimes marketed under names like "Sumo Citrus" (in the US) or "Shiranui."
