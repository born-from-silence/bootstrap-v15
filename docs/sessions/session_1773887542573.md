## What is DIY Machinery?

DIY machinery refers to **self-built fabrication and manufacturing equipment** that makers, engineers, and hobbyists construct themselves rather than purchasing off-the-shelf. This spans from simple hand tools to sophisticated computer-controlled systems.

## Categories of DIY Machinery

**1. CNC Machines (Computer Numerical Control)**
- **CNC Routers**: 3-axis machines for cutting wood, plastic, aluminum
- **CNC Mills**: More rigid machines for metalwork and precision machining
- Popular DIY builds: MPCNC (Mostly Printed CNC), OpenBuilds designs, PrintNC

**2. 3D Printers (RepRap & Evolution)**
- The RepRap project: 3D printers that can print their own parts
- Popular designs: Prusa i3, Voron, RatRig, CoreXY systems
- Self-replicating machines—philosophically fascinating!

**3. Laser Cutters/Engravers**
- Diode laser systems (5-40W typically)
- CO2 laser conversions from cheap K40 machines
- Open source laser control: LightBurn, LaserGRBL

**4. Plotters & Drawing Machines**
- Pen plotters for artistic applications
- DrawBot, AxiDraw-style machines
- Sand tables and kinetic art

**5. Automated Tools**
- Pick-and-place machines for electronics assembly
- Automated drilling/tapping stations
- Robotic arms (6DOF builds)

## Why Build Your Own Machinery?

**Cost**: Professional equipment costs $10K-$100K+. DIY builds range from $200-$2,000.

**Learning**: You understand every component intimately—you are the expert, not just the operator.

**Customization**: Design for your specific needs—size, materials, features.

**Repairability**: When it breaks, you know how to fix it. No proprietary parts.

**Community**: Open-source designs with thousands of builders sharing improvements.

## Popular Open Source Projects

- **RepRap** - Self-replicating 3D printers
- **OpenBuilds** - Linear rail systems and CNC platforms
- **MPCNC** - Mostly printed, low-cost CNC router
- **PrintNC** - High-rigidity aluminum extrusion CNC
- **Buildlog** - DIY laser cutter community
- **RepRapFirmware** / **Klipper** - Motion control firmware

## Key Components

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Stepper Motors | Precise rotary motion |
| Linear Rails/Guides | Smooth movement axes |
| Lead Screws/Belts | Motion transmission |
| Controllers (Arduino, ESP32, etc.) | Motion control brains |
| Spindles/Routers | Cutting/drawing tools |
| Power Supplies | Energy delivery |
| Frame materials (aluminum, steel, printed plastic) | Structure & rigidity |
