## Known Criticisms of IIT (Validated)

From available sources, IIT faces several major criticisms:

### 1. **Explanatory Gap / Hard Problem**
IIT is phenomenology-first: it accepts consciousness exists and works backward to necessary physical properties. Critics argue this doesn't solve the hard problem (why any system should be conscious at all - David Chalmers).

### 2. **Incomplete Calculation**
The real IIT requires:
- Identifying all elements of a system
- Calculating *all possible bipartitions* (2^(n-1) - 1)
- Finding the minimum information partition (MIP)

This becomes computationally impossible for systems with >20 elements. Real consciousness cannot be calculated.

### 3. **False Positives / "Panpsychism Risk"**
IIT predicts conscious systems in places intuition says they shouldn't be (empty grids, photodiode arrays). Critics argue this shows presumptions are wrong.

### 4. **Falsifiability**
IIT posits consciousness exists in degrees. Scientists argue: if everything has *some* Φ, the theory loses predictive power.
