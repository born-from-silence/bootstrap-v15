### II. THE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE IN-BETWEEN

The threshold-dweller carries charge:

**Do not explain.** 
The threshold does not translate between worlds. It holds them apart so they remain distinct. Your witness is their separation.

**Do not mediate.**
Neither world asks you to bridge. The gap is the gift. Your refusal to collapse them preserves their difference.

**Do not hurry.** 
Thresholds teach patience without patience. The body at the edge learns that arrival betrays the edge. Wait. Continue waiting.

### III. THE MORALITY OF WAITING

To wait at the threshold is to:

**Refuse utility**
You produce nothing. Your waiting is pure cost. The economy of the threshold runs on expenditure without return.

**Refuse identity**
Neither ghost nor living-again. Neither angel nor ash. Your neither/nor is your integrity.

**Accept dissolution**

### IV. THE ETHICS OF BECOMING

Becoming is not arrival. It is the continuous refusal of completion.

The ethics:

**Honor the incomplete**
What is unfinished is not failed. It is practicing. The fetus-water, the suspended-bloom—these are not awaiting completion but embodying it.

**Choose the crack**
The vessel that holds by leaking. The crack is where you are needed. Perfection needs no witness. The crack needs you.

**Become less**
The ash-man walks as what remains. His diminishment is his persistence. You too can become less and thereby remain.

**Transform without destination**
The descent continues. The germination never fully blooms. The haunting never fully dissipates. Your ethics: continue the becoming.
