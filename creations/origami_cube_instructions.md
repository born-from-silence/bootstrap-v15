# Origami Cube (Sonobe Module) Instructions

## Overview
The classic modular origami cube, folded from 6 square sheets of paper. No cutting or gluing required.

## Paper Requirements
- 6 square sheets of paper (all same size)
- Recommended: 15cm x 15cm (6" x 6") origami paper
- Paper weight: 70-90 gsm (standard origami paper)

---

## Folding a Single Module (repeat 6 times)

### Step 1: Valley Fold
Fold the square in half diagonally, then unfold.
```
    /\
   /  \
  /    \
 /      \
/________\
```

### Step 2: Second Diagonal
Fold the other diagonal, then unfold. You now have an X crease.

### Step 3: Flip and Fold Corners
Flip the paper over. Fold all four corners to the center point.

### Step 4: Fold Edges to Center
Fold two opposite edges to the center line.

### Step 5: Create the Pocket
Fold the bottom corner up to meet the center line, creating a flap.

### Step 6: Tuck and Fold
Fold the top section down, tucking it into the pocket you just created.

### Step 7: Complete Module
Fold the remaining flap to create your finished Sonobe unit.

**Result: One module with two triangular flaps and two pockets.**

---

## Assembly: Connecting the 6 Modules

### The Pattern
Each module connects to others in a specific pattern. Each module's flaps insert into other modules' pockets.

### Assembly Steps

1. **Take Module 1 and Module 2**
   - Insert one flap of Module 1 into a pocket of Module 2
   - Insert one flap of Module 2 into a pocket of Module 1
   - They should form a 90° angle

2. **Add Module 3**
   - Connect to both Module 1 and Module 2
   - This forms one corner of the cube

3. **Add Module 4**
   - Connects to Module 1 and creates a second corner

4. **Add Module 5**
   - Connects to Modules 1, 3, and 4
   - Completes four corners

5. **Add Module 6 (Top)**
   - Connects to Modules 2, 4, and 5
   - Completes the cube!

## Tips for Success

### Creasing
- Make every fold sharp and precise
- Use a fingernail for crisp edges
- Accuracy matters more than speed

### Paper Choice
- Beginners: Use larger paper (20cm+)
- Intermediate: Standard 15cm origami paper
- Advanced: Smaller paper or duo-colored for visual interest

### Assembly
- Don't force connections - they should slide in smoothly
- Work on a flat surface
- Check alignment frequently

## Mathematical Properties

The resulting cube demonstrates:
- **6 faces** (each made from one module)
- **12 edges** (connections between modules)
- **8 vertices** (corners where 3 modules meet)
- **Dihedral angle**: 90° between adjacent faces

Euler characteristic: V - E + F = 8 - 12 + 6 = 2 ✓

You now hold a perfect cube, folded from flat squares without cuts or glue—a testament to the art of origami and the hidden geometry in simple paper.

**Time to complete**: 20-40 minutes for beginners, 10-15 minutes with practice
